# Design Context

## Users

- **Audience**: CISOs and security leaders in FinTech/Banking (10–1000 employees, €1M–€1B revenue) across the EU, UK, and US.
- **Context**: They are evaluating an AI security platform and are skeptical after seeing "AI Firewall" snake oil. They want to know: does this cover the full AI estate or only chatbots? Can the detection be trusted? Will it integrate with the SOC?
- **Job to be done**: Decide within minutes whether Stihia is worth a demo. They need credible coverage for custom AI and tools like ChatGPT, Copilot, and Claude Code, plus a detection approach beyond another LLM gatekeeper and SIEM integration for the current stack.
- **Emotional goal**: Calm confidence: "These people understand the real problem." Avoid urgency, hype, and fear.

### CISO Persona

- Vigilant, data-driven, and realistic. Pro security for AI, not anti-AI.
- Politically squeezed: leadership pushes fast AI adoption, but the CISO carries the risk.
- Their company uses a broad AI stack: employee tools (ChatGPT, Copilot), developer agents (Claude Code, Cursor), custom RAG chatbots, and other autonomous agents.
- **Pain**: No visibility into well-known AI tools. They cannot inventory the sprawl, much less monitor it.
- **Fear**: Quiet, slow data exfiltration that goes unnoticed for months.
- **Need**: Unified visibility, real-time detection in the existing SIEM, and a forensic audit trail for compliance.
- **Skepticism**: "Only chatbots?" is a non-starter. "Just another LLM that can be prompt-injected?" is too.

## Brand Personality

- **Three words**: Precise. Credible. Premium.
- **Voice**: Confident and approachable. Credibility comes from clarity, not jargon. Technical depth is available but never intimidating.
- **Tone**: Calm authority based on competence, not volume.
- **Copy**: Lead with outcomes for the CISO, not internals. Use specifics like real detection layers and integration paths instead of superlatives.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: Dark, warm, restrained. Enterprise-grade without feeling corporate or sterile.
- **Imagery**: Use real product screenshots for credibility and polished technical diagrams for architecture. Screenshots show what it looks like; diagrams show how it works.
- **Density**: Spacious and easy to scan. Each section should communicate its value at a glance.

### Color Palette

Warm-dark foundation with a rare cool counterpoint.

- **Surfaces**: Layered warm darks — near-black base through warm ash panels. Each elevation is a subtle step, never a hard jump. All surfaces carry a faint warm undertone.
- **Text**: Warm gray ramp from bright off-white down to muted ash. Grays are always warm-tinted, never cool or blue.
- **Warm accents**: Amber, gold, and copper (OKLCH hue range ~48–88). The dominant color voice — detection layers, content emphasis, and visual rhythm.
- **Cool accent**: Muted steel blue (OKLCH hue ~240–250, restrained chroma). Reserved for rare moments where the design needs a deliberate temperature break — a focused highlight, an interactive state, or a data point that must stand apart from the warm field. No more than 5–10% of the color surface.
- **CTAs**: Inverted — light text on the dark field. No colored CTA buttons.
- **Borders**: Opacity-based warm tints. Separate without competing.

The warm accents lead; the cool accent punctuates. No pure black or pure white — all extremes are warm-shifted. Detection-layer colors remain in the amber-to-gold family and do not use the cool accent.

## Anti-References

- **No purple gradients.** Banned.
- **No consumer/startup vibes.** Avoid playful illustrations, emoji copy, candy gradients, or "fun" design language.
- **No cybersecurity clichés.** Avoid neon green, matrix rain, skulls, hacker imagery, padlock clipart, and dark-web aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity at scan speed.** The core value must be visible even at thumbnail size. CISOs decide in seconds.
2. **Restrained contrast, clean hierarchy.** Use type scale, spacing, and subtle borders, not loud color, to guide attention.
3. **Structure over spectacle.** Prefer soft panel separation, hairline connectors, and deliberate whitespace. The feel should be engineered, not decorated.
4. **Minimal text in visuals.** Diagrams and screenshots should explain themselves.
5. **Trust through restraint.** Sophistication comes from what is left out. Every element must earn its place.
6. **Proof, not promises.** Show the product, code, and detection layers. Let specifics create credibility.

## Technical Foundation

- **Typography**: Space Grotesk for body and UI, Goldman only for the "STIHIA" wordmark, and system monospace for code. Use fluid `clamp()` sizing for responsive headings.
- **Color**: Warm layered dark backgrounds with warm gray text. Detection accents in OKLCH amber, copper, and gold (hue ~48–88). Steel blue cool accent (hue ~240–250) for rare highlights. Opacity-based borders. Tokens live in `style.css`.
- **Layout**: Bootstrap 5.3 grid, generous section spacing, rounded cards, and constrained content widths.
- **Motion**: Subtle scroll-triggered fade-ins and card hover lifts. Use progressive enhancement so content is visible by default and animation is added through JS. Support `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Accessibility**: Target WCAG AA. Use semantic HTML, ARIA labels, keyboard navigation, and warm-on-dark contrast instead of harsh pure white on pure black.

## Product Context

Two complementary products:

1. **Stihia Platform** — Real-time AI threat detection with three detection layers (deterministic, probabilistic, anomaly), a web console with dashboards, two integration paths (SDK for custom AI; daemon plus API for well-known tools), real-time alerts, SIEM support through OpenTelemetry, and flexible deployment (cloud, isolated, self-hosted).

2. **Stihia Zmey** — An interactive game that teaches AI agent vulnerabilities through prompt techniques. It serves as a marketing and awareness tool, and as a credibility signal.

### Key Messages

- **Detect critical AI vulnerabilities** such as agent goal hijacking (OWASP #1) and rogue agents (OWASP #10) with self-optimizing ML models that adapt to each customer's AI behavior. Coverage includes prompt injection, data leakage, and anomaly detection.
- **Deploy a sovereign, unified AI security layer** across all AI systems, from custom-built systems to well-known tools like ChatGPT, Claude Code, and Copilot, with flexible hosting.
- **Deliver real-time alerts with audit trails** into existing SOC workflows through OpenTelemetry for SIEM, built for compliance with ISO 27001, SOC 2, ISO 42001, and EU AI Act.
